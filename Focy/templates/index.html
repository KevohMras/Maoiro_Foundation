About Us

The Youth and Drugs Project is a collaborative effort between the Villa Maraini Foundation (Italy), the Uruguayan Red Cross, the Thai Red Cross, the Kenyan Red Cross, the Red Crescent Society of Kyrgyzstan, and the Red Crescent Society of the Islamic Republic of Iran1. The project was developed to address the global threat of drug abuse, with a focus on young people. The project has three main areas of focus: capacity building activities for Red Cross and Red Crescent youth volunteers and staff, peer education in each partner country conducted by the trained volunteers, and training tools and materials on substance abuse for Red Cross and Red Crescent volunteers1.
